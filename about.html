BIO

Stefan Troschka is a sound artist and multimedia composer based in Hamburg. His works deal with the interrelations between auditory and visual spaces. His installations and pieces seem like stagings of experimental arrangements that simulate, exaggerate and reinterpret spatial perceptions. Noise music, DIY electronics and sound art form the basis of his musical practice. He is currently doing his doctorate on the perception and aesthetics of virtual-spatial sound scenes in the context of artistic research as part of the graduate program Kinetics in Sound & Space at the University of Music and Drama (HfMT) and the University of Applied Sciences (HAW) in Hamburg. He holds master degrees in Multimedia Composition and in Time-based Media/ Sound-Vision. His works have been performed at several international festivals, including Transitions (CCRMA Stanford), Next Generation (ZKM Karlsruhe), Remote Chaos Computer Club, Klub Katarakt, Greatest Hits, Blurred Edges. Stefan is a member of the duo bandschlupf for improvised electronic music and works as sound designer for the ensemble L'ART POUR L'ART and the Decoder Ensemble.
