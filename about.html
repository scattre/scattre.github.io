BIO

Stefan works in the fields of sound art, spatial composition and multimedia, often in collaboration with visual artists. He studied media technology, graduated with a thesis on musical acoustics and almost became an engineer. However, technology is not an end in itself, and so he practiced experimental sound design and spatial composition in the master's program Timebased Media Sound / Vision at HAW Hamburg. His graduate project Der Bau is an audiovisual installation with 3D sound and panoramic projection and was awarded the Karl H. Ditze Prize in 2013. In 2015 he began studying multimedia composition at the HfMT Hamburg, where his artistic works were equipped with real-time and performative components. He was engaged in computer music, live electronics and video art. Since 2019 Stefan is a PhD student in the graduate program Kinetics in Sound & Space at HAW and HfMT Hamburg, where he researches on aesthetics and perception in virtual 3D sound scenes. In addition to his academic activities, Stefan is involved in various musical projects, currently as one half of the improvising duo bandschlupf and as a member of the ensemble l'art pour l'art, where he performs live electronics for contemporary music.
